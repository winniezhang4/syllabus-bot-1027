## 3. Course Syllabus, Schedule, Delivery Mode

### Course Description
This course serves as a continuation of both CS1025A/B and CS1026A/B (ES 1036A/B engineering students)[cite: 2]. Major emphasis is placed on the organization and manipulation of abstract data types, such as lists, stacks, queues, and trees[cite: 2]. In addition, high-level language implementations of these data types are examined[cite: 2]. Object-oriented design principles are emphasized throughout the course[cite: 2]. Additional topics include sorting, searching, recursion, and analysis of algorithms[cite: 2]. Java is the programming language in use for this course[cite: 2].

### Delivery Mode
All lectures and tutorials will be delivered online[cite: 2]. There will be no in-person assessments, lectures, or labs (note, however, that the final exam will be in-person)[cite: 2].

### Learning Outcomes
Throughout the course, students will be:
* Programming in Java, an object-oriented language[cite: 2]
* Creating classes and using inheritance for sub-classes[cite: 2]
* Using and implementing abstract data types, i.e., stacks, queues, trees, lists, and linked lists[cite: 2]
* Debugging code and using exceptions for handling bugs[cite: 2]
* Programming methods from pseudo-code or a set of instructions[cite: 2]

### Course Topics
* Introduction to Java and object-oriented programming[cite: 2]
* Inheritance[cite: 2]
* Interfaces[cite: 2]
* Exceptions and Exception Handling[cite: 2]
* Collections[cite: 2]
* Linked Lists[cite: 2]
* Stacks and Queues[cite: 2]
* Ordered and Unordered Lists[cite: 2]
* Algorithm Analysis[cite: 2]
* Recursion[cite: 2]
* Trees[cite: 2]
* Sorting[cite: 2]

### Submitting Assignments
Assignments will be submitted via OWL and will be checked using Code Checking Software to ensure originality[cite: 2]. 

### Late Assignments
All assignments are due at 11:59 PM Eastern time on the assigned due date[cite: 2]. Assignments will be accepted up to four days (96 hours) after the assigned due date with a 10% deduction from the overall value per day late[cite: 2]. Assignments will not be accepted after the four-day late period[cite: 2].

### Request for Mark Adjustment
Contact the grading TA no less than five days after your grade was released[cite: 2]. The email must include:
1. Specific information regarding which assignment you wish to receive a regrade[cite: 2].
2. Why and where (in detail) you believe that you were unfairly deducted points[cite: 2].
* *Note:* If unsatisfied with the TA response, submit a request to the instructor with the subject line `<Your Course Code>: Request for Assignment Regrade`[cite: 2].

### Assignment Backups
It is the student's responsibility to keep up-to-date backups of assignment files in case of system crashes[cite: 2].

### Labs
To receive credit for a lab, you must complete the work as explained in the instructions and submit the completed files on OWL by the specified due date each week[cite: 2]. Labs that are not submitted on time without accommodations will not be accepted (receive a 0)[cite: 2]. Accommodated missed labs will have their weight pushed to the final exam[cite: 2].

### Participation
There will be three participation events worth a total of 5%, announced on OWL:
1. Introduction post (1%)[cite: 2]
2. Midterm exam review video (2%)[cite: 2]
3. Final exam review video (2%)[cite: 2]

### Midterm and Final Exam
* **Midterm Exam:** Held online. Tentative date is Monday, July 8th[cite: 2].
* **Final Exam:** Held in-person. Date announced closer to the end of term[cite: 2].
* **Proctoring:** Exams will use the remote proctoring service, Proctortrack[cite: 2]. Video face scans and photo identification are required[cite: 2].
* **Passing Requirements (Strict Rule):** To receive a passing grade (50%) in the course, your mark on the final exam must be at least 45%, and your weighted average on the assignments must be at least 45%[cite: 2]. Otherwise, the maximum overall mark you can receive is 45%[cite: 2]. To get a grade of 65% or higher, your final exam mark must be at least 50%, and your assignment average must be at least 50%[cite: 2].

## 6. Student Absences

### Accommodated Evaluations
For work totaling 10% or more of the final course grade, valid medical or supporting documentation must be provided to the Academic Counselling Office of your Faculty[cite: 2]. 

### Missed Assignments
Assignments will not be re-weighted, nor will the grade be shifted to another course component[cite: 2]. An alternate due date will be arranged if approved by Academic Counselling[cite: 2].

### Missed Tutorial Assignment
Missed tutorial assignments cannot be re-weighted[cite: 2]. The two lowest tutorial assignment grades will be dropped to account for illness or compassionate considerations[cite: 2].

### Missed Final Examination
Contact Academic Counselling to assess eligibility to write a Special Examination (makeup final exam)[cite: 2].
